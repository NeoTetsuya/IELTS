How is TPACK Used?

According to Koehler and Mishra (2009), TPACK is a conceptual tool to guide teachers in integrating technology in pedagogically sound and content-appropriate ways. It is used to:

  • Design and deliver effective instruction by helping educators select technologies that align with the subject matter and teaching strategies.
  • Evaluate and reflect on teaching practice, enabling teachers to assess how well they are integrating digital tools.
  • Inform teacher training and professional development by identifying knowledge gaps for meaningful technology use.

Example: In Online or Hybrid Teaching

In an online IELTS speaking class, students might use Flipgrid to record answers (technology). This allows for asynchronous practice and peer feedback (pedagogy) while targeting specific exam preparation goals (content).

"Effective teaching with technology requires an understanding of the representation of concepts using technologies; pedagogical techniques that use technologies in constructive ways to teach content; [and] knowledge of what makes concepts difficult or easy to learn and how technology can help redress some of the problems that students face."

- Koehler & Mishra (2009, p. 67)

Case Study: GSP in Mathematics

GSP is a dynamic geometry software that allows users to construct and manipulate mathematical objects. In one study, pre-service secondary mathematics teachers used GSP to:

  • Explore and construct geometric objects: Drawing regular polygons, lines, circles, graphs of functions, etc.
  • Design inquiry-based learning activities: Students could drag points and shapes to observe patterns (e.g., see that the sum of angles in a triangle is always 180°).
  • Teach lessons using visual and interactive elements: Dynamically demonstrating concepts instead of drawing static figures on a board.

The Goal: Developing TPACK

This approach helped pre-service teachers develop the three core knowledge areas in an integrated way:

  • Content Knowledge (CK): Knowing the subject (mathematics).
  • Pedagogical Knowledge (PK): Knowing how to teach (inquiry-based methods).
  • Technological Knowledge (TK): Knowing how to use the tech tool (GSP).

Your Role as a TESOL Teacher

Master the 3 Core Knowledge Areas

  • Content (What you teach?): Deepen your understanding of English language systems.
  • Pedagogy (How you teach?): Use methods like communicative language teaching, task-based learning, etc.
  • Technology (Which tools you use?): Learn how and when to use tools like Quizizz, Padlet, or ChatGPT effectively.

Integrate Technology Purposefully

Use applications based on learners’ needs and lesson objectives. Select appropriate tools to develop particular skills. For example, use Elsa Speak to allow shy students to practice speaking at home.

Design Lessons at the Intersection of TPACK

Intentionally connect the three core components (CK, PK, TK) when designing tasks and activities.

Reflect and Adapt

Continuously update and adapt tools to be suitable for different students (ages, proficiency levels) and learning contexts.

Content Knowledge (CK)

This is the teacher's expertise in the subject matter itself. For TESOL, it includes a deep understanding of grammar, phonology, vocabulary, syntax, and language skills.

×

Pedagogical Knowledge (PK)

This refers to the teacher's knowledge about how students learn and how to teach. It includes understanding teaching methods (e.g., task-based learning), assessment strategies, and error correction, as well as how to organize students, manage behavior, and use time efficiently.

×

Technological Knowledge (TK)

This is a teacher's familiarity with digital tools and resources. It's not just knowing *about* tools like Zoom, Kahoot!, or Padlet, but also knowing *how* to operate them effectively.

The 7 Components of TPACK

CK (Content Knowledge): Subject matter expertise (e.g., grammar, phonology, vocabulary).

PK (Pedagogical Knowledge): Understanding how students learn and how to teach (e.g., task-based learning, error correction).

TK (Technological Knowledge): Familiarity with digital tools (e.g., Zoom, Kahoot!, Quizlet, Padlet).

PCK (Pedagogical Content Knowledge): How to teach the subject effectively.

TCK (Technological Content Knowledge): How technology can change the way the subject is taught.

TPK (Technological Pedagogical Knowledge): How teaching changes with tech tools (e.g., flipped classroom, blended learning).

TPACK (Technological Pedagogical Content Knowledge): The integration of all three knowledge domains to create effective, technology-supported teaching.
